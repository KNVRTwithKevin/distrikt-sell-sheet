How to sell DISTRIKT.A cheat sheet to close meetings.

Every client is on a path to the same destination: a business where AI is not bolted on, but built in. We call it AI-Native by Design. That path runs through three tiers. Clients can enter at any point, and your job is to spot which one fits.

Most companies have deployed AI. Almost none have built a system. The difference is design. A deployed tool does what you tell it. Designed AI infrastructure learns the business, compounds over time, and acts on its behalf.

"You don't need another AI tool. You need the system that makes the AI you already bought finally pay off. DISTRIKT builds that system, from a single live workflow to a fully autonomous revenue engine, with an entry point for every company."

Data Privacy Model

Standard vs. Enterprise Isolated

Available at Tier 2 and Tier 3. Not a separate product, just a modifier on how client data interacts with the DISTRIKT intelligence network.

Objection "I don't want my data shared."
The answer is simple: they don't have to. They pay the enterprise rate, their data stays sovereign, and they still get the benefit of every deployment that came before them. The system still helps them, they just don't add to it. Here are the two ways it can work:

Standard Engagement

Standard tier rates

Client data flows through the KNVRT intelligence layer. Anonymized solution patterns are extracted and contribute to the cross-industry network. The client benefits from every prior deployment, every pattern and learning DISTRIKT has run before them.

Enterprise Isolated

Premium multiplier (DISTRIKT scopes)

Client data stays fully private in their own environment. Zero contribution to the network. They still query against the anonymized intelligence DISTRIKT has accumulated across all other deployments, so they benefit from the network without contributing to it. Same model the enterprise LLM providers use.

Objection Handling

The pushback you'll hear, and the answer

Objection "Why not just hire an AI engineer to build this?"
They can. The engineer will connect the data and build something that works. But in four months they'll have a system that only knows their company. DISTRIKT runs on a proprietary architecture refined across dozens of industries, on top of an intelligence network that gets smarter with every engagement. You're not selling the build. You're selling everything we've learned running it across every client before them.
Objection "We already have AI tools, we just aren't using them well."
That is exactly Tier 1. The license is live, the ROI isn't. We come in and build the first live workflow inside the stack they already pay for, in 30 days, fixed fee. Low lift, fast proof, then the roadmap shows them what's next.
Objection "This sounds like a big commitment."
It doesn't have to start big. Clients can enter at any tier. Most start with a fixed-fee Tier 1 Sprint to see proof before anything larger. The destination is the same; the entry point is wherever they're comfortable.
Objection "If everything runs through your AI, is anyone actually managing my media?"
Yes. KNVRT does the reasoning and reporting; our team still makes every optimization by hand, budget shifts, platform moves, campaign setup, executed by a dedicated specialist. The AI makes us faster and sharper. It doesn't replace the human running your account.
Gatekeeper "We can build this ourselves."
Also voiced as: "too many projects in motion to bring in a third party," or "we'd rather go slow, build vs. buy." Who says it: Head of AI, AI/automation leads, CTO, IT, AI engineers, who may feel their turf or role is threatened.

How to handle: Don't win it in the room. Route up to the economic buyer. Amplify, don't threaten, position DISTRIKT as giving their team more bandwidth and making them the hero. Then flag it to DISTRIKT to handle the build-vs-buy conversation. Rep rule: flag, don't debate. Avoid blockers, book the call.
